Urban-rural linkages and SDG interlinkages in Ghana

Mensah, A.M., Okyere, S.A., Owusu, G., Kita, M.

Sustainability, 12(18), 7369

This study examines the interlinkages between urban and rural areas in Ghana and their implications for achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). We identify key leverage points where interventions can maximize co-benefits across multiple goals, particularly focusing on food systems, water resources, and infrastructure development. Our analysis reveals that integrated urban-rural planning is essential for achieving sustainable development in Ghana.

Disaster risk reduction education in Ghanaian schools

Apronti, P.T., Osamu, S., Otsuki, K., Mensah, A.M.

Sustainability, 7(9), 11999-12019

This research evaluates the integration of disaster risk reduction (DRR) education in Ghanaian schools. Through surveys and curriculum analysis, we assess the current state of DRR education and identify gaps in knowledge dissemination. The study proposes a comprehensive framework for enhancing DRR education that incorporates local knowledge and community-based approaches.

54 citations 2016

Community-based environmental education for migrant youth

Mensah, A.M., Kuranchie, F.A.

Journal of Education and Learning, 5(4), 73-84

This paper explores the effectiveness of community-based environmental education programs for migrant youth in Ghana. Using participatory action research, we demonstrate how culturally responsive environmental education can enhance ecological awareness and foster sustainable practices among vulnerable populations. The study highlights the importance of integrating local ecological knowledge with formal education approaches.

42 citations 2018

Coastal vulnerability assessment in Ghana using GIS

Mensah, A.M., Addo, K.A., Wiafe, G.

Ocean & Coastal Management, 156, 167-178

This study develops a coastal vulnerability index (CVI) for Ghana's coastline using geographic information systems (GIS) and multi-criteria analysis. We integrate physical, ecological, and socio-economic parameters to identify hotspots of vulnerability to sea-level rise and coastal erosion. The research provides a decision-support tool for coastal zone management and climate adaptation planning.
